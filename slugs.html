Sea Sheep

Costasiella kuroshimae

This leafy little guy is part of the family of Sacoglossans, which are mollusks and slugs that eat algae. What's interesting about this specific type of slug is that it's able to photosynthesize! It does this by retaining the chloroplasts from the food it eats. This process is known as kleptoplasty. In some cases, sea sheep can live on this process alone for months on end.
